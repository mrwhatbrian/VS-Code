The Human Body

Cells

Our human body is made up of special building blocks called CELLS. Our bodies are made up of TRILLIONS of cells and these cells have very specific jobs.

  • Muscle cells help you move
  • Nerve cells in the brain help you think
  • Red blood cells transport the oxygen we breathe to all the parts of the body that need it
  • White blood cells help us to fight illnesses
  • Most cells are very small. HUNDREDS of cells could fit on the point of a pin.

Tissues

When many of these special cells work together to perform a specific function it is called a TISSUE (not like the tissue you blow your nose on though)!

There are 4 main kinds of tissues:

  • Muscle tissue: Helps you move
  • Nervous tissue: Found in the brain and spinal cord, allowing communication among other tissue types
  • Epithelial tissue: Found in the skin and airways
  • Connective tissue: Tendons (connects muscle to bone), Ligaments (connect bone to bone), Cartilage (helps form joints)

Organs

When different tissues work together, an organ is formed such as the heart, liver, lungs, kidneys, and even the skin.

Skin: The largest organ in the human body, composed of three layers:

  • Epidermis: The outer layer, forming a protective barrier
  • Dermis: Contains blood vessels, nerve tissue, and muscle tissue responsible for goosebumps
  • Subcutaneous layer: Consists mainly of fat, cushioning the skin and providing protection from cold temperatures

Skeletal System

The main role of the skeletal system is to provide support for the body, protect delicate internal organs, and provide attachment sites for the organs.

  • Skull
  • Spinal Cord
  • Rib Cage
  • Arm bones, wrists, hands, fingers
  • Leg bones, ankles, feet, toes

Muscular System

The main role of the muscular system is to provide movement. Muscles work in pairs to move limbs and provide the organism with mobility. Muscles also control the movement of materials through some organs, such as the stomach, intestine, and heart.

Circulatory System

The main role of the circulatory system is to transport nutrients, gases (such as oxygen and CO2), hormones, and wastes through the body.


Heart: The heart is an organ and a muscle located a little to the left of your chest. It pumps blood around your body, providing oxygen and nutrients while removing waste.

Respiratory System

The respiratory system helps us breathe by taking in oxygen and expelling carbon dioxide.


Lungs: Large organs protected by the rib cage, working with the diaphragm to enable breathing.

Experiment: Adult vs. Child Lung Capacity

Digestive System

The digestive system breaks down food and absorbs nutrients necessary for grow align-flex-centerth and maintenance.

  • Mouth: Chews food, saliva begins digestion
  • Stomach: Contains acids to break down food further
  • Small intestine: Absorbs nutrients, with help from the pancreas, liver, and gallbladder
  • Large intestine: Absorbs water and forms solid waste

Nervous System

The nervous system relays electrical signals, directing behavior and controlling physiological processes such as digestion and circulation.

Urinary/Excretory System

The excretory system filters out cellular wastes, toxins, and excess water or nutrients from the circulatory system.

Lymphatic/Immune System

The immune system destroys and removes microbes and viruses. The lymphatic system removes excess fluids and fat from the blood.

Endocrine System

The endocrine system relays chemical messages through the body, helping control processes such as nutrient absorption and grow align-flex-centerth.

Reproductive System

The reproductive system allows humans to reproduce by manufacturing cells for reproduction.
